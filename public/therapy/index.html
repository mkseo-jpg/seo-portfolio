2025-08-11

Case Study 3#

A client in the counselling and therapy space got in touch with me - she was struggling to get consultations and wanted me to help her with her bookings. At the time she was running a static, one-page website; I proposed a complete redesign on WordPress as a foundation for building traffic from SEO, followed by a keyword content strategy to target searchers who may have an interest in her services.

I built and designed the new site from scratch, created seven main optimised service pages each based around a high-intent keyword (eg self-esteem counselling, anxiety counselling, relationship therapy, etc), and then a dedicated ‘Learning & Resources’ blog section for content. Defined three main keyword clusters which related loosely to the 7 service pages (one of them also mapped to a local keyword, ‘London’ was the modifier) and mapped a list of keywords and search intents to several potential posts within each cluster, with the idea of supporting the rankings of the main service pages with topical relevancy and also bringing in qualified traffic via their own keywords. Initial performance was strong on search (sitewide), but we could see that it wasn’t converting into enquiries at a good enough rate. So we designed CTAs for each blog post and service page for a free 10-minute discovery call, which saw enquiries skyrocket. The site currently brings, with the top blog post bringing in ~500 clicks in the 3 months from May to August 25 (roughly 1/3 of traffic), and 1500 clicks over the last 3 months in general. With a conversion rate of roughly 3-4%, this client has seen a huge uptick in enquiries and a full calendar as a result of the SEO works I handled

Parallel to this, I embarked upon a backlink campaign to secure listings, citations and references from industry-leading publications, which helped with E-E-A-T

I also secured a top 3 ranking in the google maps pack for the local keyword (‘gestalt therapy London’)
